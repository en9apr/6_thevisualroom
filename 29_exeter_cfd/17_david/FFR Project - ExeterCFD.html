FFR Project

From ExeterCFD
Jump to: navigation, search

FFR is Fractional Flow Reserve, a measure of the occlusion in an artery due to coronary heart disease. This is an experimental measurement based on measuring the pressure on either side of the occlusion using a pressure probe introduced through the femoral artery (an unpleasant and dangerous procedure). The objective of the project was to see whether this could be replaced by a computational procedure of MRI scan, Image Based Meshing and CFD. This has been done in the past using high fidelity CFD; our aim was to see if the computational time could be significantly reduced by using lower quality simulation (e.g. steady state rather than transient simulation) and still get the same results.

The work was funded by an Innovation Voucher which paid for a postgrad research assistant, David Tranter. Some reports; on the methodology and results from a sample of patients.

A journal paper has been prepared but has so far not been accepted for publication.

Heather Bolt's summer research project on ANN as surrogates for CFD was inspired by this project


This is a presentation made to Prof Eric Paterson by David Tranter when he visited.
